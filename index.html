Home
Welcome to my website, where you can freely browse my thoughts and see what projects I'm working on. My name's Brian Strobel and I'm a student at UW. My current priority is to get accepted into the computer science program here, so I'm starting this website to keep track of all the projects I've done, and distribute them in a smooth, polished way (although this website doesn't look very smooth right now).

I have some ambitious goals for game development that I will probably still be pursuing 10 years from now. I've always been interested in the MMORPG genre, however I lack the vital skills and resources needed to implement my idea, so I've planned out several smaller projects that will get me where I need to be. In particular I am going to attempt to use Google's FireBase to implement a turn based online game, which will be hosted on this website.

I am also going to start keeping a blog. Topics will be about whatever feel like discussing, but it's going to be largely centered on game development. I'd like to "chart my progress" so that A: I can look back and laugh at my former self, and B: other people who are currently in my position will have a guide and could possibly use my blog as a starting point for their game-development-skill-getting.

Helping others down the murky roads of game development is especially important to me because if you ask about multiplayer game development on Stack Exchange or any other website, you get shut down immediately; their answer is always "You can't. No. Don't do it. Impossible." This was one of the main reasons I was discouraged from game development in high school, so I hope that people will listen to the advice on my blog, instead of the advice from Stack Exchange to give up.

Okay, I'm done rambling. Since I will be updating this soon, no one is ever going to read this anyway...
